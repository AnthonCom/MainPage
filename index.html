A Cotterell Overview

Hello! As you saw, my name is Anthony. I've had a keen interest in computing and IT spanning all the way back to my childhood, using the family computer to edit .shp files for the original Homeworld (1999). Sadly I had limited ability to develop this interest further until my early 20s, when I could finally afford to build a system with my own choice of hardware. I've now built multiple systems for myself over the years, as well as building systems for family and friends. These range from office builds running early AMD Ryzen series CPUs with the most basic components, up to my current personal device that may be falling victim to Intel's 13th & 14th gen CPU fumble.

I found myself with an opportunity at the start of the year to further expand my knowledge to include software skills as well as hardware. I have, in the past, attempted programming projects, though these were simple modifications for games rather than creating from scratch. Undertaking a "skills bootcamp", I've learned the fundamentals for HTML & CSS, Javascript, SQL and Python development. These skills have allowed me to create a number of personal projects.

What can I offer you as a potential employee? More than you might think. My development knowledge is Junior level at best right now, but I can bring a wealth of experience from outside the Development sector that most Junior Developers won't have. I've worked in multiple business sectors over the years, and picked up a wide range of compatible skills. Imagine a new hire with more than 10 years of customer service experience, knowledge of GDPR intricacies, tech-based Operations and management experience, AND is willing to build up their development skills from scratch with the aid of bug fixing and testing experience learned through messing around in the file structures of heavily modded videogames.

To any prospective recruiters, please be aware that I am diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder, attention deficit disorder and anxiety. Special accommodations may be required because of these disabilities. My communications preferences are via text (e-mail primarily), I would prefer initial contact in this format to allow me to process information and prepare a response at my own pace.
